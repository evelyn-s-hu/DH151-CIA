About the Map

Hate crimes were sorted into five categories - Anti-Race, Anti-Gender, Anti-Ableism, Anti-Religion and Anti-Sexual Orientation hate crimes. On the map, Anti-Race cases are in red, Anti-Gender cases are in green, Anti-Sexual Orientation cases are in blue, Anti-Abelism cases are in pink and Anti-Religion cases are in purple. Each category is a layer on the map which can be toggled on and off. When the desired layer is toggled on, click on the city to view the breakdown of hate crimes by specific motivations (e.g. anti-Asian, anti-gay).

Key Finding 1: There are significantly more Anti-Race and Anti-Sexual Orientation hate crimes.

Across the United States, the number of hate crimes are not equally distributed among the five main categories of hate crimes. Notably, there are significantly more Anti-Race and Anti-Sexual Orientation hate crimes than the other three categories.

Key Finding 2: The five different categories of hate crimes are not equally distributed across cities.

While large cities are more likely to have higher rates of hate crimes in general, some cities such as Los Angeles, New York City and Boston have large numbers of all five categories of hate crimes, while others such as Dallas, San Diego and Philadelphia have a high number of hate crimes for certain categories but not others. This suggests that population density alone is not enough to explain high levels of hate crime and that other underlying social tensions and dynamics should be explored.

Data Critique

While it may seem like there are more hate crimes happening on the West and East coasts than the Midwest or Central U.S., this could be due to the fact that the map only reflects hate crimes that were officially reported and recorded by authorities. We acknowledge that hate crimes are statistically underreported, especially for certain groups at risk, and that this map would not be able to represent those hate crimes accurately.

Sources

We used two sources: the United States Hate Crimes (1991-2018) Dataset detailed all the hate crimes in our maps from 2010 to 2018, which we then assigned longitude and latitude to each city using US Cities Database.
